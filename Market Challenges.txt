Market Challenges:
The market needs for open fault circuit detection systems comes from their potential to address various challenges faced by the electrical department.
1.	Human and Animal safety risk: Open conductor faults pose a direct risk of electrocution to humans and animals, especially in rural or agricultural areas where power lines may traverse fields, farms, or grazing lands.
2.	Fire Hazards and Wildfire Prevention: Open conductors, particularly those in contact with dry vegetation or other combustible materials, are a major source of fire hazards
3.	Delayed Fault Detection and Prolonged Outages: In conventional systems, open conductor faults often result in low fault currents, which may not trigger traditional protection mechanisms
4.	Utility Liability and Financial Losses: Utilities face significant legal and financial liabilities due to fires caused by open conductors or electrocution incidents.
5.	Unbalanced Load Conditions and Power Quality Issues: Open conductor faults often result in unbalanced load conditions that can negatively impact power quality and stability in distribution networks.
6.	Remote and Hard-to-Reach Areas: Power lines in agricultural and rural areas often span large distances and are located in hard-to-reach regions, making it difficult for utility crews to manually inspect and detect faults.
